# WhatsApp Best Practices

## Phone Quality Rating

WhatsApp rates your business phone based on user actions (blocks, reports). A low quality rating that doesn't improve over time will **decrease your messaging limit**.

Monitor blocks/reports via the **Insights tab** in WhatsApp Manager.

## Opt-In Requirements

- All users must **actively consent** to receive WhatsApp messages before you contact them
- Opt-in language must explicitly state the user is agreeing to receive messages from your business **over WhatsApp**
- Send a **confirmation message** after collecting opt-in consent

## Messaging Best Practices

| Practice | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| Channel name | Reflect your brand so users recognize it as legitimate |
| Timing | Send messages during appropriate times |
| Confirmation | Always confirm opt-in consent after collection |

## Opt-Out Strategy

Opt-outs do **not** impact phone quality rating — opt-outs are preferable to blocks/reports.

**Recommended approach:**
- Include opt-out instructions in the **footer of your first message** (e.g., "Reply STOP to unsubscribe")
- Regularly include the opt-out footer in future campaigns

This trades a lost subscriber for a preserved quality rating.

## Why This Matters

| User Action | Impact on Quality Rating |
|-------------|--------------------------|
| Block | Negative |
| Report | Negative |
| Opt-out | No impact |

Consistently following opt-in requirements and providing clear opt-out paths keeps blocks/reports low and protects your messaging limits.
